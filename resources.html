FAQ

1. We can do it ourselves. Why you?

Features are not a managed process. We add SLAs, owners, weekly reviews, A/B copy, waitlist rules, rollback. That moves KPIs.

2. Our PMS already has reminders.

We use it if helpful. Uplift comes from self-reschedule, waitlist backfill, timing and copy, and SLA discipline.

3. Is this legal under PECR/GDPR?

Yes. Service messages with explicit consent, clear opt-outs, no marketing content. DPIA and data minimisation.

4. Do you integrate with our EHR?

No. The pilot is No EHR by design. Admin layer only. No PHI.

5. Do you handle PHI?

No. We do not collect, process, or store PHI.

6. Who must be involved on our side?

Named owners for inbox, reminders/waitlist, no-show recovery, and charge-day end (if insurer). One training and a weekly review.

7. What if staff push back?

Reversible steps, clear scripts, one-click rollback. Less manual work, not more. We adjust weekly.

8. What if KPIs don’t move?

If 2 of 3 targets are not reached in 30–45 days, a credit against the retainer applies.

9. What does success look like?

Hit any two: TAT −40%; DNA −3–5pp; cost/booking −20%. Before/after is shown weekly.

10. Deposits and cancellations?

Fair 24–48h window. Deposits applied to prime-time or new patients where appropriate. Exceptions for vulnerable groups.

11. Can we start in one location?

Yes. We recommend single-site pilot, then scale.

12. Internal time required?

Set-up days 1–10 with one training, then a 30-minute weekly review. Day-to-day effort usually drops.

13. Do you support PMI/RCM front-end?

Yes. In the pilot when PMI ≥30% or pre-auth present (RCM-lite). Ongoing cadence under Standard/Plus retainers.

14. Why do you ask if insurer share is ≥30%?

To select scope with the best 30–45-day ROI. <30% insurer → we prioritise DNA/TAT/cost/booking and keep lite SOP for pre-auth. ≥30% or pre-auth present → we enable RCM-lite to move charge-lag/DSO.

15. Security basics?

Least-privilege access, audit log, DPIA, DPAs with processors, no PHI, rollback on request.
