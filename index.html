Why this matters

Running a clinic means juggling diaries, calls, referrals and billing while keeping care front and centre. We remove friction in the admin lane so teams stay focused on patients and revenue moves without new headcount.

How it works — Core-4 pilot

1. Assess

Confirm scope, owners and SLAs; capture four weeks of baseline KPIs across DNA, FRT/TAT and cost per booking.

2. Configure

Enable reminders with self-reschedule, waitlist backfill, shared inbox, voicemail→transcription and deposits where needed.

3. Launch

Go live in 10 days with service messages and pay-links operating with explicit consent and clear scripts.

4. Optimise

Weekly WBR, A/B copy and rule adjustments. Branch into Self-pay or Insurer/RCM-lite by payer mix or pre-authorisations.

Core-4 in plain words

Reminders + self-reschedule + waitlist

Patients confirm or move slots in two clicks; empty slots go to a live waitlist for backfill.

Shared inbox + SLA

One queue for email + SMS/WhatsApp with tags, macros and first-response targets to keep FRT under four hours.

No-show recovery

Automatic nudges after a missed appointment with a reschedule link and optional deposit trigger for prime-time.

Voicemail → transcription → draft

Missed calls become text tasks with suggested replies so nothing sits unheard overnight.

If insurer context (PMI ≥30% or pre-authorisations present), we activate RCM-lite inside the pilot: same-day charge-out, eligibility/pre-auth inbox, daily submit, denials worklist. No EHR. No PHI.

Am I a fit?

Ideal profile

  • ≥600 visits/month (GP/derm/psych) or ≥400 scans/month (diagnostics).
  • DNA ≥8% or first-response time >4 hours.
  • OK to run outside PMS/RIS (exports/inbox); service SMS/WhatsApp allowed.
  • If PMI ≥30% or pre-authorisations present → Insurer/RCM-lite track is included.

Not a fit if…

  • DNA <5% and FRT ≤4 hours already.
  • Service SMS/WhatsApp not permitted by policy.
  • No owners, no SLAs, or “best-effort only”.
  • Strict “PMS-only, no external tools” rules in place.
