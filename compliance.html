Trust by design

No EHR. No PHI. Operational workflows only.

EpicRose operates at the administrative layer with GDPR, DPA, DPIA and PECR front of mind. Service messages stay within consent, data is minimised, and every change can be rolled back instantly.

GDPR / DPA / DPIA

DPIA for every pilot

Each engagement has a documented Data Protection Impact Assessment covering purpose, lawful basis and risk mitigation.

Data minimisation

We process only contact and scheduling context needed to deliver service messages and booking changes.

DPA with processors

All inbox, SMS/WhatsApp, forms and payment providers operate under signed DPAs with sub-processor transparency.

Retention & deletion

Data is retained only as long as required to deliver the pilot scope, then archived or deleted per agreement.

Cookies & tracking

During the pilot we avoid marketing cookies entirely. Operational tools use functional cookies only; any analytics is opt-in and anonymised.

PECR service messages

Explicit consent

Consent copy: “I agree to receive service messages (SMS/WhatsApp/email) about my appointments, scheduling and payments. These are not marketing messages. I can opt out at any time by replying STOP or via the link provided.”

Opt-out & audit

Opt-out processed immediately across all channels. Audit logs capture who enabled copy, when consent was obtained and when opt-outs were actioned.

Security controls

Least privilege

Access scoped to the operational team; shared inbox and automation tooling use individual accounts with MFA.

Rollback on request

Every change has a rollback path if staff need to revert scripts, templates or automations.

No PHI

EpicRose operates at the administrative layer only. We do not collect, process or store personal health information (PHI).

Operational disclaimer

We are an operational service, not a clinical provider. No diagnosis, treatment or clinical decisions are made.

Need compliance materials?

We provide DPIA templates, consent copy and processor lists during onboarding. Request them via the screener or intro call.
