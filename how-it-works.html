Pilot overview

30-day pilot, week-by-week

A defined sprint to install the Core-4 operating rhythm: reminders with self-reschedule, waitlist backfill, shared inbox with SLAs, voicemail-to-text drafts and optional insurer/RCM-lite track. Nothing drifts—every step has owners, SOPs and rollback.

  • Days 1–2 — Kick-off & baselines

    Confirm owners for inbox, reminders/waitlist, no-show recovery and charge-day end (if insurer track). Lock four SLAs and gather four weeks of KPIs.

  • Days 3–4 — Shared inbox + SLAs

    Unified queue, tags, macros and call-back list. SLA report: first response ≤4h in business hours; missed-call call-back rules.

  • Days 5–6 — Reminders + self-reschedule + waitlist

    Service SMS/WhatsApp/email with consent; secure links; A/B copy and timing. Waitlist auto-offers for prime-time and late cancellations.

  • Day 7 — No-show recovery

    Automated sequence with 2–3 nudges and a reschedule link; optional deposit trigger for prime-time slots.

  • Day 8 — Voicemail → transcription → draft

    Missed calls become tasks with suggested replies so the inbox closes daily.

  • Day 9 — Dashboard & training

    Live KPI board; 45–60 minute training; handover of SOPs, templates and scripts.

  • Day 10 — Weekly Business Review

    Adjust copy, timing and rules to hit the targets. Branch next: Self-pay track (pay-links, no-show recovery tuning) or Insurer/RCM-lite (eligibility/pre-auth inbox, same-day charge-out, daily submit, denials worklist).

What we need from you

Owners & cadence

  • Name leads for inbox, reminders/waitlist, no-shows and an RCM lead if the insurer track is active.
  • One 45–60 minute team training to walk through the Core-4 playbook.
  • Weekly 30-minute review (WBR) to adjust copy and rules together.

Enablers

  • Enable consent copy for service SMS/WhatsApp; opt-out live from day one.
  • Export access or shared inbox permissions outside the PMS/RIS to run the service layer.
  • Baseline data (DNA%, FRT/TAT, touches per booking; charge-lag/DSO if insurer context).

Day-to-day workload usually drops once reminders, waitlist and inbox cadences go live.

What we don’t do in the pilot

  • No EHR integration or migration.
  • No clinical content or clinical workflows.
  • No PHI collection, processing or storage.
  • No vendor lock-in—tools remain replaceable.

SLA & Pilot charter

Four SLAs

  • Calls answered or returned ≤60s.
  • First response ≤4h within business hours.
  • Same-day backfill for released slots.
  • Charge-day end <24h (insurer track).

Targets & rollback

  • Named owners per stream and a rollback button for any change.
  • Hit any two of three in 30–45 days: FRT/TAT −40%; DNA −3–5pp; cost per booking −~20%.
  • KPI-credit applies if fewer than two targets are met (see Pricing).

Technology note

We use rules, no-code/RPA and light AI assistance where it helps. Everything runs in the admin lane—no EHR access, no PHI.
